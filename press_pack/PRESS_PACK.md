Day 3: The lightest build of the week. The highest-value concept.

Every AI system I've built this week ingests data, sanitises it, and processes it.
But none of it means anything if the output doesn't sound like YOUR firm.

The solution: identity files. Two Markdown documents.
`identity.md` — firm persona, tone, style guide, preferred vocabulary
`compliance_rules.md` — prohibited language, required disclaimers, MiFID II rules

Here's what I mean. Same prompt. Two outputs:

**Without identity files:**
"The fund has performed well recently and looks like a good investment opportunity..."

**With identity files:**
"Our analysis suggests the fund's risk-adjusted return profile remains compelling, supported by disciplined duration management and selective credit positioning. Capital at risk. Past performance is not a reliable indicator of future results."

One sounds like a chatbot. One sounds like your CIO wrote it.

The files are human-editable Markdown.
No code change needed to update firm voice.
Every agent in the system loads them on startup.
